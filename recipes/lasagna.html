Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a layered pasta dish typically made with wide, flat pasta sheets. To prepare, a meat or vegetable ragu (sauce) is made by simmering ground beef or vegetables with tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs. Separately, a béchamel sauce (white sauce made from butter, flour, and milk) is prepared. In a baking dish, layers of pasta, ragu, béchamel, and cheese (usually mozzarella and Parmesan) are alternated. The dish is baked in the oven until the top is golden and bubbly. Lasagna is known for its rich, hearty flavor and creamy texture.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna pasta sheets
  • Ground beef or vegetables (for vegetarian)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Oregano or Italian herbs
  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Milk
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook lasagna pasta sheets until al dente, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a pan, sauté onions and garlic in olive oil, then add ground beef or vegetables and cook until browned.
  4. Add tomato sauce, salt, pepper, and herbs. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. In a separate pan, melt butter, add flour, and stir to make a roux. Gradually whisk in milk to make béchamel sauce.
  6. In a baking dish, layer pasta sheets, meat sauce, béchamel, and cheese.
  7. Repeat layers, finishing with mozzarella and Parmesan on top.
  8. Bake for 30-40 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  9. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before serving.
