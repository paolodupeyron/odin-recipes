Ice Cream

Description

Ice cream is a sweet frozen dessert made by mixing cream, milk, sugar, and flavorings (like vanilla or chocolate). This mixture is then churned in an ice cream maker to incorporate air and prevent ice crystals from forming, creating a smooth, creamy texture. Ice cream can be flavored with a variety of ingredients like fruits, chocolate, or nuts, and is a classic treat served in cones, bowls, or alongside desserts.

Ingredients

  • Heavy cream
  • Whole milk
  • Sugar
  • Vanilla extract (or other flavorings)
  • Egg yolks (optional, for richer ice cream)
  • Salt (a pinch)

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, heat cream, milk, and sugar over medium heat until the sugar dissolves.
  2. Add vanilla extract or other flavorings.
  3. If using egg yolks, temper them by slowly adding the hot milk mixture, then cook until slightly thickened.
  4. Chill the mixture in the fridge for at least 2 hours.
  5. Pour the chilled mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  6. Transfer to a container and freeze until firm, about 4 hours.
